Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about CDAC entrance exam, courses, admission process, and training institutes in Pune.

General Questions

CDAC (Centre for Development of Advanced Computing) is a premier R&D organization of the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Government of India. It offers various Post Graduate Diploma courses in advanced computing through its authorized training centers across India. These courses are designed to bridge the gap between academia and industry requirements in the IT sector.

CDAC courses offer several advantages:

  • Industry-relevant curriculum designed by experts
  • Hands-on practical training with real-world projects
  • Excellent placement opportunities with top companies
  • Government-recognized certification
  • Affordable fees compared to many private institutions
  • Strong alumni network across the IT industry

Each CDAC institute in Pune has its own strengths:

  • CDAC ACTS Pune: Being the flagship institute, it offers the most comprehensive infrastructure, experienced faculty, and excellent placement records. It's ideal for students looking for the complete CDAC experience.
  • Sunbeam Pune: Known for its practical approach to teaching and strong industry connections. It's great for students who want focused training with good placement support.
  • IACSD Pune: Offers a good balance of theory and practical training with affordable fees. It's suitable for students looking for quality education at a slightly lower cost.
  • Sunbeam Karad: Located outside Pune city, it offers quality education at lower fees with lower living expenses. It's ideal for budget-conscious students.

The "best" institute depends on your priorities - location, budget, specific course offerings, and personal preferences. We recommend comparing them based on our detailed profiles and visiting them if possible before making a decision.

The basic eligibility criteria for most CDAC courses are:

  • Bachelor's degree in any discipline with Mathematics as a subject in 10+2 or at graduation level
  • For engineering graduates: B.E./B.Tech in any branch
  • For non-engineering graduates: BCA, BCS, B.Sc. (Computer Science/IT/Electronics/Mathematics/Statistics/Physics), MCA, M.Sc. (Computer Science/IT/Electronics/Mathematics/Statistics)
  • Minimum 50% marks in qualifying degree (45% for reserved categories)
  • Final year students can also apply, subject to completion of their degree before admission

Note: Specific courses may have additional eligibility requirements. For example, PG-DESD may prefer candidates with electronics background.

Entrance Exam Questions

C-CAT (CDAC Common Admission Test) is the entrance examination for admission to various PG Diploma courses offered by CDAC. It is conducted three times a year, typically in:

  • February/March (for March batch)
  • June/July (for September batch)
  • November/December (for February batch)

The exact dates are announced on the official CDAC website. The exam is conducted online at designated centers across India.

The C-CAT exam consists of three sections:

  • Section A: Logical Reasoning, English, Quantitative Aptitude, and Computer Fundamentals (Mandatory for all candidates)
  • Section B: Computer Programming and Computer Science (Mandatory for all candidates)
  • Section C: Advanced Mathematics and Computer Science (Optional, but recommended for specific courses like DBDA)

Key points about the exam:

  • Each section is of 90 minutes duration
  • Multiple-choice questions with negative marking (0.25 marks deducted for wrong answers)
  • Total marks depend on the sections attempted (A+B = 200 marks, A+B+C = 300 marks)
  • Online computer-based test

Here's a comprehensive preparation strategy for C-CAT:

  • Understand the syllabus: Thoroughly go through the official syllabus for each section.
  • Section A preparation:
    • Logical Reasoning: Practice puzzles, coding-decoding, blood relations, etc.
    • English: Focus on grammar, vocabulary, reading comprehension
    • Quantitative Aptitude: Cover basic mathematics, percentages, profit-loss, etc.
    • Computer Fundamentals: Study basic computer concepts, hardware, software, networking
  • Section B preparation:
    • Programming concepts: Variables, loops, conditions, functions, arrays
    • Data structures: Arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees
    • Algorithms: Sorting, searching, basic complexity analysis
    • C/C++/Java fundamentals
  • Section C preparation (if attempting):
    • Advanced Mathematics: Probability, statistics, linear algebra, calculus
    • Advanced Computer Science: DBMS, OS, computer networks, software engineering

Recommended resources:

  • Previous years' question papers (available on the CDAC website)
  • Standard aptitude books (R.S. Aggarwal, Arun Sharma)
  • Programming books (Let Us C, Data Structures by Tanenbaum)
  • Online practice platforms (HackerRank, LeetCode, GeeksforGeeks)
  • Mock tests (available on PrepWizard and other platforms)

Time management is crucial during the exam. Practice solving questions within time constraints to improve your speed and accuracy.

A "good" score in C-CAT depends on the course you're targeting and the competition level in that particular batch. However, as a general guideline:

  • For top institutes (CDAC ACTS, Sunbeam Pune):
    • PG-DAC: 140+ out of 200 (Sections A+B)
    • PG-DBDA: 210+ out of 300 (Sections A+B+C)
    • PG-DITISS: 130+ out of 200 (Sections A+B)
  • For other institutes (IACSD, Sunbeam Karad):
    • PG-DAC: 120+ out of 200
    • PG-DBDA: 180+ out of 300
    • PG-DITISS: 110+ out of 200

Note: These are approximate scores based on previous trends and may vary. The actual cutoffs depend on the difficulty level of the exam and the number of applicants in a particular batch.

CDAC uses a percentile-based ranking system, so your relative performance compared to other candidates is more important than the absolute score.

Yes, there is negative marking in the C-CAT exam:

  • For each correct answer: +1 mark
  • For each incorrect answer: -0.25 mark (25% negative marking)
  • For unattempted questions: No marks deducted

Due to the negative marking, it's advisable to:

  • Attempt questions you're confident about
  • Skip questions you're completely unsure of
  • If you can eliminate 2-3 options in a 4-option MCQ, it might be worth taking a calculated risk

Admission Process Questions

The complete admission process for CDAC courses involves the following steps:

  1. Registration for C-CAT: Apply online on the CDAC website during the application period.
  2. C-CAT Examination: Appear for the entrance test at your chosen center.
  3. Result Declaration: Results are typically announced within 2-3 weeks after the exam.
  4. Counseling Registration: Register for the centralized counseling process by paying the required fee.
  5. Choice Filling: Submit your preferences for courses and centers in order of priority.
  6. Seat Allocation: Seats are allocated based on your rank, preferences, and availability.
  7. Fee Payment: Pay the course fee as per the schedule to confirm your admission.
  8. Document Verification: Submit and verify your original documents at the allotted center.
  9. Course Commencement: Start your course as per the schedule.

Important points to note:

  • There might be multiple rounds of counseling to fill vacant seats
  • All official communications happen through the CDAC website and registered email
  • Missing any deadline can result in cancellation of candidature
  • Keep checking the official CDAC website for updates and announcements

The following documents are typically required for CDAC admission:

  • C-CAT admit card and score card
  • Seat allocation letter
  • Fee payment receipt
  • 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates
  • Graduation mark sheets (all semesters/years)
  • Graduation degree certificate or provisional certificate
  • Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC/EWS), if applicable
  • PWD certificate, if applicable
  • Photo identity proof (Aadhar Card/PAN Card/Driving License/Passport)
  • Passport-sized photographs (usually 4-6)
  • Migration certificate (if required by the institute)
  • Character certificate from the last attended institution (if required)

Important notes:

  • Carry both original documents and self-attested photocopies
  • Some institutes may have additional document requirements
  • Final year students who haven't received their degree yet should carry a certificate from their college/university stating that they have completed all requirements for the degree

Changing your institute or course after allocation is possible but subject to certain conditions:

  • During counseling rounds: If you're allocated a seat in the first round but prefer a different institute/course, you can participate in subsequent rounds for upgradation. However, you must accept the currently allocated seat by paying the required fee to be eligible for upgradation.
  • After final allocation: Once the counseling process is complete, transfers between institutes or courses are generally not allowed. Each institute operates independently, and there's no formal transfer mechanism between CDAC centers.
  • Special cases: In exceptional circumstances (medical reasons, etc.), you may submit a formal request to CDAC headquarters, but approval is at their discretion and rarely granted.

Important advice:

  • Be very careful while filling your preferences during counseling
  • Research all institutes and courses thoroughly before making choices
  • Only include those options in your preference list that you're willing to join
  • If you're unsure about joining a particular institute/course, it's better not to include it in your preferences

Yes, CDAC follows the Government of India's reservation policy for admissions. The reservation categories and percentages are:

  • Scheduled Caste (SC): 15%
  • Scheduled Tribe (ST): 7.5%
  • Other Backward Classes (OBC-NCL): 27%
  • Economically Weaker Sections (EWS): 10%
  • Persons with Disabilities (PWD): 5% horizontal reservation across all categories

Important points regarding reservation:

  • Candidates must possess valid category certificates as per Government of India norms
  • OBC candidates must have a non-creamy layer (NCL) certificate issued within the last year
  • EWS candidates must have a valid EWS certificate for the current financial year
  • PWD candidates must have a disability certificate showing at least 40% disability
  • Category certificates are verified during the admission process

There might be relaxation in qualifying marks or cutoffs for reserved category candidates as per government norms.

Missing any step in the admission process can have serious consequences:

  • Missing counseling registration: If you don't register for counseling within the specified timeline, you won't be considered for seat allocation despite having a valid C-CAT score.
  • Missing choice filling: If you don't submit your preferences during the choice filling period, you won't be allocated any seat.
  • Missing fee payment deadline: If you don't pay the required fee after seat allocation within the given timeframe, your allocation will be canceled, and the seat will be offered to other candidates in subsequent rounds.
  • Missing document verification: If you don't report for document verification at the allotted institute, your admission may be canceled.

In case of genuine emergencies:

  • Contact the CDAC admission cell immediately via email or phone
  • Explain your situation with supporting evidence (medical certificates, etc.)
  • Request for consideration or extension, if possible

However, please note that extensions or special considerations are at the discretion of CDAC authorities and are granted only in exceptional circumstances. It's always advisable to adhere to all deadlines and complete all processes on time.

Courses & Curriculum Questions

CDAC offers several Post Graduate Diploma (PG) courses in various domains of advanced computing. The main courses are:

  • PG-DAC (Post Graduate Diploma in Advanced Computing): Focuses on software development, web technologies, and application development. This is the most popular and widely offered course.
  • PG-DBDA (Post Graduate Diploma in Big Data Analytics): Covers data science, machine learning, big data technologies, and analytics.
  • PG-DITISS (Post Graduate Diploma in IT Infrastructure, Systems and Security): Focuses on network administration, system administration, and cybersecurity.
  • PG-DESD (Post Graduate Diploma in Embedded Systems Design): Covers embedded programming, IoT, and hardware interfacing.
  • PG-DVLSI (Post Graduate Diploma in VLSI Design): Focuses on VLSI design, verification, and testing.
  • PG-DASSD (Post Graduate Diploma in Advanced Secure Software Development): Covers secure coding practices and application security.
  • PG-DIOT (Post Graduate Diploma in Internet of Things): Focuses on IoT technologies, protocols, and applications.
  • PG-DAIML (Post Graduate Diploma in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning): Covers AI algorithms, deep learning, and their applications.

Note: Not all courses are offered at all centers. The availability of courses varies across different CDAC institutes. The most commonly available courses at Pune centers are PG-DAC, PG-DBDA, PG-DITISS, and PG-DESD.

  • PG-DESD:
    • Specialized field with specific job opportunities

Current trends (as of 2023):

  • PG-DBDA and PG-DAIML are seeing increased demand due to the boom in data science and AI
  • PG-DITISS has stable demand with the growing focus on cybersecurity

The "best" course depends on your background, interests, and career goals. Rather than just following market trends, choose a course that aligns with your aptitude and interests for long-term career satisfaction.

CDAC courses are intensive and fast-paced, but they are designed to be accessible to graduates from various backgrounds, including non-CS graduates. Here's what you should know:

Difficulty Level:

  • Rapid progression from basics to advanced concepts

For Non-CS Graduates:

Tips for Non-CS Graduates:

Success stories: Many non-CS graduates have successfully completed CDAC courses and built rewarding careers in IT. The initial learning curve might be steeper for non-CS graduates, but with persistence, the gap narrows significantly by the end of the course.

Evaluation Process:

  • Regular progress reviews and demonstrations

The project work is highly valued by employers and often becomes a significant talking point during job interviews. Many students have secured job offers based on their project work. It provides practical experience in applying the concepts learned throughout the course and working in a team environment.

The placement process at CDAC institutes typically follows these steps:

Pre-Placement Phase:

  1. Resume preparation workshops
  2. Aptitude test training
  3. Mock interviews and group discussions
  4. Technical interview preparation
  5. Soft skills and communication training

Placement Process:

  1. Company Registration: Companies register with the placement cell and share their requirements.
  2. Pre-Placement Talk: Companies conduct presentations about their organization, roles, and expectations.
  3. Application Process: Interested students apply for specific companies.
  4. Shortlisting: Companies shortlist candidates based on criteria like academic performance, skills, etc.
  5. Selection Process: Typically includes:
    • Aptitude/technical test
    • Coding test (for development roles)
    • Group discussion (sometimes)
    • Technical interview
    • HR interview
  6. Job Offer: Selected candidates receive offer letters.

Placement Policies:

  • Most institutes follow a "one student, one job" policy
  • Once placed, students may not be allowed to sit for further companies
  • Some institutes have salary thresholds for this policy (e.g., if placed below 5 LPA, you can appear for higher-paying companies)
  • Attendance at pre-placement talks is often mandatory
  • Students must maintain professional conduct throughout the process

Timing of Placements:

  • Placements typically begin in the last 1-2 months of the course
  • Some companies may visit earlier for pre-placement offers
  • Placement activities may continue for 1-2 months after course completion

Note: The exact placement process may vary slightly across different CDAC institutes. It's advisable to check the specific policies of your institute during orientation.
